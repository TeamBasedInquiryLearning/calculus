Observation 2.2.16.

We have two ways to compute analytically the derivative at a point. For example, to compute \(f'(1)\text{,}\) the derivative of \(f(x)\) at \(x=1\text{,}\) we have two methods
  1. We can directly compute \(f'(1)\) by finding the difference quotient on the interval \([1,1+h]\) and then taking the limit as \(h\to 0\text{.}\)
  2. We can first find the derivative function \(f'(x)\) by computing the difference quotient on the interval \([x,x+h]\text{,}\) then taking the limit as \(h\to 0\text{,}\) and finally evaluating the expression for \(f'(x)\) at the input \(x=1\text{.}\)
The latter approach is more convenient when you want to consider the value of the derivative function at multiple points!
