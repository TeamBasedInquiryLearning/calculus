Fact 7.4.9.
The slope of a polar curve \(r=f(\theta)\)is \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=\displaystyle\frac{dy/d\theta}{dx/d\theta}=\displaystyle\frac{f^\prime(\theta)\sin(\theta)+f(\theta)\cos(\theta)}{f^\prime(\theta)\cos(\theta)-f(\theta)\sin(\theta)}\text{,}\) provided that \(dx/d\theta\neq 0\) at \((r,\theta)\text{.}\) Vertical tangents occur when \(dy/d\theta=0\) and \(dx/d\theta\neq 0\text{;}\) horizontal tangents occur when \(dx/d\theta=0\) and \(dy/d\theta\neq 0\text{.}\)
