Theorem 3.5.7. The First Derivative Test.

Suppose that \(x=c\) is a critical point of \(f(x)\) and that \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  1. If \(f'(x) > 0\) before \(x=c\) and \(f'(x) < 0\) after \(x=c\text{,}\) then a local maximum occurs at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f'(x) < 0\) before \(x=c\) and \(f'(x) > 0\) after \(x=c\text{,}\) then a local minimum occurs at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  3. If \(f'(x)\) is the same sign on both sides of \(x=c\text{,}\) then neither a local maximum nor a local minimum occur when \(x=c\text{.}\)
