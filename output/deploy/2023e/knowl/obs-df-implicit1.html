Observation 2.7.1.

Many of the equations that has been discussed so far fall under the category of an explicit equation. An explicit equation is one in which the relationship between \(x\) and \(y\) is given explicitly, such as \(y = f(x)\text{.}\) In this section we will examine when the relationship between \(x\) and \(y\) is given implicity. An implicit equation looks like \(f(x,y) = g(x,y)\) where both sides of the equation may depend on both \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)
