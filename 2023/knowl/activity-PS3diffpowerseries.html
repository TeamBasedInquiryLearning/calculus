Activity 9.3.1.

How might we use the known geometric power series

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{1-x}=1+x+x^2+x^3+x^4+\dots \end{equation*}

to find the value of

\begin{equation*} 1+2x+3x^2+4x^3+\dots\text{?} \end{equation*}

(a)

Which operation describes the relationship between these two series?

  1. Bifurcation

  2. Composition

  3. Differentiation

  4. Multiplication

(b)

What is the result of applying this operation to \(\frac{1}{1-x}\text{?}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle 0\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{(1-x)^2}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle 1-\frac{1}{x}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \frac{x}{1-x^2}\)
