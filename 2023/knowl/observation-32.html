Observation 5.6.7.

To find integrals like \(\int \frac{x^2+x+1}{x^3+x} dx\) and \(\int\frac{10x-11}{x^2+x-2}\text{,}\) we'd like to decompose the fractions into simpler partial fractions that may be integrated with these formulas

\begin{equation*} \int\frac{1}{x+b}dx=\ln|x+b|+C \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{1}{x^2+b^2}dx=\frac{1}{b}\arctan\left(\frac{x}{b}\right)+C \end{equation*}
