Activity 5.8.18.

Consider when \(p=1\text{.}\) Then \(\frac{1}{x^p}=\frac{1}{x}\) and \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^p}\ dx=\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x}\ dx=\ln|x|+C\text{.}\)
(a)
What can we conclude about \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x} dx\text{?}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x} dx\) converges.
  2. \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x} dx\) diverges.
  3. There is not enough information to determine whether this integral converges or diverges.
(b)
What can we conclude about \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x} dx\text{?}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x} dx\) converges.
  2. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x} dx\) diverges.
  3. There is not enough information to determine whether this integral converges or diverges.
