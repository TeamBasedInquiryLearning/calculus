Use similar reasoning to complete the following proof that \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\arcsin(x)\right]=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{Let }1-x^2&=1-\unknown\theta=\unknown\theta\\ x^2&=\unknown\\ x&=\unknown\\ dx&=\unknown\,d\theta\\ \theta&=\unknown \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \,dx&=\int\frac{1}{\sqrt{\unknown}}\,(\unknown\,d\theta)\\ &= \int d\theta\\ &= \unknown + C\\ &= \arcsin(x) + C \end{align*}
