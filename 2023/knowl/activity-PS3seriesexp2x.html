Activity 9.3.3.

Suppose we wish to find the power series for the function \(f(x)=e^{2x}\) by modifying the power series \(\exp(z)=e^z=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{z^n}{n!}.\)
(a)
Substituting \(z=2x\text{,}\) what is the power series for \(\exp(2x)\text{?}\)
  1. \(\exp(2x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{2x^n}{n!}=2+2x+x^2+\frac{1}{3}x^3+\dots\text{.}\)
  2. \(\exp(2x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{2x^{n+1}}{n!}=2x+2x^2+x^3+\frac{1}{3}x^4+\dots\text{.}\)
  3. \(\exp(2x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(2x)^n}{n!}=1+2x+2x^2+\frac{4}{3}x^3+\dots\text{.}\)
  4. \(\exp(2x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{(2n)!}=1+\frac{x}{2}+\frac{x^2}{4}+\frac{x^3}{720}+\dots\text{.}\)
(b)
What is the interval of convergence for \(x\) for this series?
  1. \(\left(-\infty,\infty\right)\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \left(-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right)\text{.}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \left(0,\frac{1}{2}\right)\text{.}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \left(-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]\text{.}\)
