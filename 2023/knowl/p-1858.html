Suppose we let \(x=\sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Then \(\frac{dx}{d\theta}=\cos(\theta)\) and \(dx=\cos(\theta) d\theta\text{.}\) Which of the following best represents the integral \(\displaystyle \int \sqrt{1-x^2}dx\text{?}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int \sqrt{1-\sin^2(\theta)}dx\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int \sqrt{ 1-\sin^2(\theta)\cos(\theta)}d\theta\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int \sqrt{ 1-\sin^2(\theta)}\cos(\theta)d\theta\)
