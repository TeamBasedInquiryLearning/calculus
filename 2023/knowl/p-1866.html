Complete the following trignometric substitution to find \(\displaystyle\int\frac{7}{x\sqrt{9x^2-16}}\,dx\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{Let }9x^2-16&=\unknown\theta-16=\unknown\theta\\ 9x^2&=\unknown\\ x&=\unknown\\ dx&=\unknown\,d\theta\\ \theta&=\unknown \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \int\frac{7}{x\sqrt{9x^2-16}}\,dx &=\int\frac{7}{\unknown\sqrt{\unknown}}\,(\unknown\,d\theta)\\ &= \int \unknown d\theta\\ &= \unknown + C\\ &= \frac{7}{4}\arcsec(\frac{3}{4}x) + C \end{align*}
