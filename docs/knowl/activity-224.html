Activity 4.9.15.

When \(x=2\text{,}\) we know that \(u=2^4+4=20\text{.}\) Likewise when \(x=0\text{,}\) we know that \(u=0^4+4=4\text{.}\) Use these facts to complete the below computation.

\begin{align*} \int_0^2 \frac{x^3}{x^4+4}\,dx &&\text{Let }&u=x^4+4\\ &&& du = 4x^3\,dx\\ &&& \frac{1}{4} du = x^3\,dx\\ \int_{x=0}^{x=2} \frac{x^3}{x^4+4}\,dx &= \int_{u=\unknown}^{u=\unknown} \frac{1/4}{u}\, du\\ &= \dots\\ &= \frac{1}{4}\ln(20)-\frac{1}{4}\ln(4) \end{align*}
