Activity 3.1.14.

Consider a cup of coffee initially at \(100^\circ\)F. The said cup of coffee was forgotten this morning in my living room where the thermostat is set at \(72^\circ\)F. I also observed that when I initially prepared the coffee, the temperature was decreasing at a rate of \(3.8\) degrees per minute.

(a)

In the long run, what temperature do you expect the coffee to tend to? Use this information in the model \(y=ae^{-kt}t+c\) to determine the value of \(c\text{.}\)

(b)

Using the initial temperature of the coffee and your value of \(c\text{,}\) find the value of \(a\) in the model \(y=ae^{-kt}t+c\text{.}\)

(c)

The scenario also gives you information about the value of the rate of change at \(t=0\text{.}\) Use this additional information to determine the model \(y=ae^{-kt}t+c\) completely.

(d)

You should find that the temperature model for this coffee cup is \(y= 72 + 38 e^{-0.1t}\text{.}\) Explain how the values of each parameter connects to the information given.
