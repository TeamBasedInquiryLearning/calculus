    Section 6.1 Average Value (AI1)

    Learning Outcomes

    • Compute the average value of a function on an interval.

    Subsection 6.1.1 Activities

    Activity 6.1.1.

    Suppose a car drives due east at 70 miles per hour for 2 hours, and then slows down to 40 miles per hour for an additional hour.
    (a)
    How far did the car travel in these 3 hours?
    1. \(110\) miles
    2. \(150\) miles
    3. \(180\) miles
    4. \(220\) miles
    (b)
    What was its average velocity over these 3 hours?
    1. \(55\) miles per hour
    2. \(60\) miles per hour
    3. \(70\) miles per hour
    4. \(75\) miles per hour

    Activity 6.1.2.

    Suppose instead the car starts with a velocity of \(30\) miles per hour, and increases velocity linearly according to the function \(v(t)=30+20t\) so its velocity after three hours is \(90\) miles per hour?
    (a)
    How can we model the car‚Äôs distance traveled using calculus?
    1. Integrate velocity, because position is the rate of change of velocity.
    2. Integrate velocity, because velocity is the rate of change of position.
    3. Differentiate velocity, because position is the rate of change of velocity.
    4. Differentiate velocity, because velocity is the rate of change of position.
    (b)
    Then, which of these expressions is a mathematical model for the car‚Äôs distance traveled after 2 hours?
    1. \(\displaystyle \int (30+20t)\,dt\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \int (30t+10t^2)\,dt\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \int_0^3 (30+20t)\,dt\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \int_0^3 (30t+10t^2)\,dt\)
    (c)
    How far did the car travel in these 3 hours?
    1. \(110\) miles
    2. \(150\) miles
    3. \(180\) miles
    4. \(220\) miles
    (d)
    Thus, what was its average velocity over three hours?
    1. \(55\) miles per hour
    2. \(60\) miles per hour
    3. \(70\) miles per hour
    4. \(75\) miles per hour

    Observation 6.1.3.

    To obtain the average velocity of an object traveling with velocity \(v(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) we may find its distance traveled by calculating \(\int_a^b v(t)\text{.}\) Thus, the average velocity is obtained by dividing by the time \(b-a\) elapsed:
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{b-a}\int_a^b v(t)\,dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
    For example, the following calculuation confirms the previous activity:
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{3-0}\int_0^3 (30+20t)\,dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Definition 6.1.4.

    Given a function \(f(x)\) defined on \([a,b]\text{,}\) it‚Äôs average value is defined to be
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{b-a}\int_a^b f(x) \,dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Activity 6.1.5.

    (a)
    Which of the following expressions represent the average value of \(f(x)=-12 \, x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4\) over the interval \([-1, 2]\text{?}\)
    1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{3}\int_{-1}^{2}\left(x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4 \right) dx\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{-1}{1}\int_{1}^{2}\left(x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4 \right) dx\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{2}\int_{1}^{2}\left(x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4 \right) dx\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{-1}{4}\int_{-1}^{2}\left(x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4 \right) dx\)
    (b)
    Show that the average value of \(f(x)=-12 \, x^{2} + 8 \, x + 4\) over the interval \([-1, 2]\) is \(-12\text{.}\)

    Activity 6.1.6.

    (a)
    Which of the following expressions represent the average value of \(f(x)=x\cos(x^2)+x\) on the interval \([\pi, 4\pi]\text{?}\)
    1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{3\pi}\int_{0}^{4\pi}\Big(x\cos(x^2)+x\Big) dx\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{0}^{4\pi}\Big(x\cos(x^2)+x\Big) dx\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{3\pi}\int_{\pi}^{4\pi}\Big(x\cos(x^2)+x\Big) dx\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\pi}^{4\pi}\Big(x\cos(x^2)+x\Big) dx\)
    (b)
    Find the average value of \(f(x)=x\cos(x^2)+x\) on the interval \([\pi, 4\pi]\) using the chosen expression.

    Activity 6.1.7.

    Find the average value of \(\displaystyle g(t)=\frac{t}{t^2+1}\) on the interval \([0, 4]\text{.}\)

    Activity 6.1.8.

    A shot of a drug is administered to a patient and the quantity of the drug in the bloodstream over time is \(q(t)=3te^{-0.25t}\text{,}\) where \(t\) is measured in hours and \(q\) is measured in milligrams. What is the average quantity of this drug in the patient‚Äôs bloodstream over the first 6 hours after injection?

    Activity 6.1.9.

    Which of the following is the average value of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,8]\text{?}\)
    Note \(f(x)=\begin{cases} 1, & 0\leq x\leq 3 \\ 4, & 3 < x \leq 6 \\ 2, & 6 < x \leq 8 \end{cases}\text{.}\)
    1. \(\displaystyle 4\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{7}{3}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 19\)
    5. \(\displaystyle 2.375\)
