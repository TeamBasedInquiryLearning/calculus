Activity 5.4.2.

To find \(\displaystyle \int \sqrt{9-4x^2} \,dx\text{,}\) we will need a more advanced substitution. Which of these candidates is most reasonable?
  1. Let \(v\) satisfy \(9-4x^2=9-9e^{2v}=9e^{-2v}\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(\theta\) satisfy \(9-4x^2=9-9\sin^2\theta=9\cos^2\theta\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(w\) satisfy \(9-4x^2=4-8\ln|w|=4\ln|2w|\text{.}\)
  4. Let \(\phi\) satisfy \(9-4x^2=4-4\cos^2\phi=4\sin^2\phi\text{.}\)
