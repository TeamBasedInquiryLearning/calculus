Remark 2.2.6.

To specify the indendent variable of our function, we say that \(f'(x)\) is the derivative of \(f(x)\) with respect to \(x\text{.}\) For the derivative function of \(y=f(x)\) we also use the notation:
\begin{equation*} f'(x)=y'(x)=\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{df}{dx}. \end{equation*}
The last type of notation is known as differential (or Leibniz) notation for the derivative.
