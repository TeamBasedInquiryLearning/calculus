Activity 8.4.4.

Consider the infinite series

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n. \end{equation*}

(a)

What of the following approaches will best help us determine the convergence of this series?

  1. Noticing that \(\displaystyle 3\cdot\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\text{.}\)

  2. Noticing that \(\displaystyle 2+\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\text{.}\)

  3. Noticing that \(\displaystyle 2n+\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\text{.}\)

  4. Noticing that \(\displaystyle 2(0.7)^0+2(0.7)^1+2(0.7)^2+\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\text{.}\)

  5. Noticing that \(\displaystyle 0+1+2+\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\text{.}\)

(b)

Using your chosen approach, determine if \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=3}^\infty 2(0.7)^n\) converges, and if so, to what it converges.
