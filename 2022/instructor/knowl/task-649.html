Task 7.2.1.d.

It follows that when \(t=2\text{,}\) \(\frac{dx}{dt}=2\) and \(\frac{dy}{dt}=4\text{.}\) Which of the following conjectures seems most likely?

  1. The slope \(\frac{dy}{dx}=2\) could also be found by computing \(\frac{dx}{dt}+\frac{dy}{dt}\text{.}\)

  2. The slope \(\frac{dy}{dx}=2\) could also be found by computing \(\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}\text{.}\)

  3. The slope \(\frac{dy}{dx}=2\) is always equal to \(\frac{dx}{dt}\text{.}\)

  4. The slope \(\frac{dy}{dx}=2\) is always equal to \(\frac{dy}{dt}\text{.}\)
