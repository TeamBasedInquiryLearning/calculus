Consider a cylindrical tank filled with water, where the base of the cylinder has a 3 meter radius, and a height of 10 meters. Consider a 2 m thick slice of water sitting 6 meters high in the tank. Using the fact that the mass of this water is \(1000\cdot \pi (3)^2\cdot 2=18000\pi\) kg, estimate how much work is needed to lift this slice 4 more meters to the top of the tank.

  1. \(18000\pi\cdot 4\) Nm.

  2. \(18000\pi\cdot 9.8\) Nm.

  3. \(18000\pi\cdot 4\cdot 9.8\) Nm.

  4. \(18000\pi\cdot 6\) Nm.

  5. \(18000\pi\cdot 6\cdot 9.8\) Nm.
