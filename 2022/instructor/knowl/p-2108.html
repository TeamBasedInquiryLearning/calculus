Consider the plots of \(f(x), g(x), h(x) \) where \(0 < g(x) < f(x) < h(x)\text{.}\)

If \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 f(x) dx\) is convergent, what can we say about \(g(x), h(x)\text{?}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 g(x) dx\) and \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 h(x) dx\) are both convergent.

  2. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 g(x) dx\) and \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 h(x) dx\) are both divergent.

  3. Whether or not \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 g(x) dx\) and \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 h(x) dx\) are convergent or divergent cannot be determined.

  4. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 g(x) dx\) is convergent and \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 h(x) dx\) is divergent.

  5. \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 g(x) dx\) is convergent and \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 h(x) dx\) can either be convergent or divergent.
