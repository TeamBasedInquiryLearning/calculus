Definition 2.5.5.

When looking at the composite function \(f(g(x))\text{,}\) we have that

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}\Big(f( g(x) )\Big)= f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x). \end{equation*}

Using differential notation, if we consider the composite function \((v \circ u)(x)\text{,}\) we have that

\begin{equation*} \frac{dv}{dx}= \frac{dv}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \text{.} \end{equation*}
This defined the chain rule.
