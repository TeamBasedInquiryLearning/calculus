Item 5.

We have

  • \(\displaystyle r(x)=3 \, \cos\left(x\right) \cdot x\)

  • \(\displaystyle r'(x)=-3 \, x \sin\left(x\right) + 3 \, \cos\left(x\right)\)

  • \(\displaystyle r''(x)=-3 \, x \cos\left(x\right) - 6 \, \sin\left(x\right)\)

  • \(\displaystyle r'''(x)=3 \, x \sin\left(x\right) - 9 \, \cos\left(x\right)\)

  • \(\displaystyle r^{(iv)}=3 \, x \cos\left(x\right) + 12 \, \sin\left(x\right)\)

The functions involved repeat every four derivatives. So the twelth derivative should have the summand \(3 \, x \cos\left(x\right)\text{.}\) The second summand has a coefficient that increases by 3 every time, so the twelth derivative should have coefficient 36. Putting this together \(r^{(xii)}=3 \, x \cos\left(x\right) + 36 \, \sin\left(x\right)\)
