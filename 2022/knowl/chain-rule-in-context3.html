Activity 2.5.16.

Suppose that a fish population at \(t\) months is approximated by

\begin{equation*} P(t) = 100 \cdot 4^{0.05 t} \end{equation*}

(a)

Find \(P(10)\) and use units to explain what this value tells us about the population.

(b)

Find \(P'(10)\) and use units to explain what this value tells us about the population. You can use the approximation \(\ln(4) = 1.4\text{.}\)
