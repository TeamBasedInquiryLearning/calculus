Theorem 1.1.11.

Suppose that:

  • for an interval around \(x=a\text{,}\) we have that \(f(x) \leq g(x) \leq h(x) \) ;

  • the limit as \(x\) approaches \(a\) of \(f(x) \) is equal to the same value \(L\) as the limit of \(h(x)\text{,}\) so \(\lim_{x\to a} f(x) = L =\lim_{x\to a} h(x) .\)

Then, the limit of \(g(x)\) as \(x \to a\) is also \(L\text{,}\) so \(\lim_{x\to a} g(x) = L \)
