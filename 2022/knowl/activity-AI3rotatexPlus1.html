Activity 6.3.1.

Consider the function \(f(x)=x+1\) and the solid of revolution generated by rotating the region bound by:

\begin{align*} 0&\leq y \leq f(x)\\ 0& \leq x \leq 6 \end{align*}

about the \(x\)-axis.

We wish to find the volume of this solid.

(a)

Suppose we estimate the volume of this solid by approximating it with two cylinders of “height” \(\Delta x=3\) and radii \(f(0)=1, f(3)=4\text{:}\)

What is the volume of the cylinder with radius \(f(0)=1\text{?}\)

  1. \(\pi\cdot 0^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  2. \(\pi\cdot 1^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi\cdot 3^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi\cdot 4^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  5. \(\pi\cdot 6^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  6. \(\pi\cdot 7^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

(b)

What is the volume of the cylinder with radius \(f(3)=4\text{?}\)

  1. \(\pi\cdot 0^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  2. \(\pi\cdot 1^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi\cdot 3^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi\cdot 4^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  5. \(\pi\cdot 6^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

  6. \(\pi\cdot 7^2\cdot 3\text{.}\)

(c)

Suppose we now use a “height” of \(\Delta x=2\text{:}\)

Fill out the following table:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|} \hline x_i & \Delta x & \text{Radius of Cross Section}=f(x_i) & \text{Estimated Volume}\\ \hline x_1=0 & 2 & & \\ \hline x_2=2 & 2 & & \\ \hline x_3=4 & 2 & & \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

(d)

Which of the following Reimann sums best approximates the volume of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi x_i^2\Delta x\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi (x_i+1)^2\Delta x\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum 2\pi x_i\Delta x\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum 2\pi (x_i+1)\Delta x\)

(e)

Which of the following integrals computes the volume of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^6 \pi x^2dx\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^6 \pi (x+1)^2dx\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^6 2\pi xdx\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^6 2\pi (x+1)dx\)
