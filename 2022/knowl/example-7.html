Example 5.1.17.

Here is how one might write out the explanation of how to find \(\int_1^3 x^2e^{x^3}\,dx\) from start to finish by transforming bounds into terms of \(u\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int_1^3 x^2e^{x^3}\,dx &&\text{Let }&u=x^3\\ &&&du = 3x^2\,dx\\ &&&\frac{1}{3}du = x^2\,dx\\ \int_1^3 x^2e^{x^3}\,dx &= \int_{x=1}^{x=3} e^{(x^3)} (x^2\,dx)\\ &= \int_{u=1^3}^{u=3^3} e^{u} \frac{1}{3}du\\ &= \left[\frac{1}{3}e^{u}\right]_{1}^{27}\\ &= \frac{1}{3}e^{27} - \frac{1}{3}e \end{align*}
