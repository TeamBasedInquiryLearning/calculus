Let \(f(x)\) be a function such that:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|} f(-2) & f'(-2) & f''(-2) & f'''(-2) & f^{(4)}(-2) & f^{(5)}(-2) & f^{(6)}(-2) \\ \hline 0 & 2 & -16 & 54 & -128 & 250 & -432 \end{array} \end{equation*}

  1. Find a Taylor polynomial for \(f(x)\) centered at \(x=-2\) of degree \(3\text{.}\)

  2. Using the table above, find a general closed form for \(f^{(n)}(-2)\text{.}\)

  3. Use (b) to find a Taylor series for \(f(x)\) centered at \(x=-2\text{.}\)
