Task 3.9.2.a.
Notice that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to 0} \frac{e^{2+x}-e^2}{x}\) is the derivative of \(e^x\) at \(x=2\) (where \(x\) was used for \(h\)). Given this observation, what is this limit equal to?
  1. 2
  2. \(\displaystyle e\)
  3. \(\displaystyle e^2\)
  4. The limit does not exist.
