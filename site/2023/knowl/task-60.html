Task 1.5.5.a.
Graph the following functions and consider their limits as \(x\) approaches positive and negative infinity. Which function(s) have a limit that is nonzero and constant? Find each of these limits.
  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle f(x)= \frac{x^3-x+3}{2x^3-6x+1} \)
  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle f(x)= \frac{x^2-3}{5x^3-2x^2+5} \)
  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle f(x)= \frac{x^4-3x-2}{3x^3-5x+1} \)
  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle f(x)= \frac{10x^5-3x+2}{5x^5-3x^2+1} \)
  5. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle f(x)= \frac{-8x^2-5x+1}{2x^2-2x+3} \)
