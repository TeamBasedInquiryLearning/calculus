    Chapter 1 Limits (LT)

    Learning Outcomes

    How do we measure “close-by” values?
    By the end of this chapter, you should be able to...
    1. Find limits from the graph of a function.
    2. Infer the value of a limit based on nearby values of the function.
    3. Compute limits of functions given algebraically, using proper limit properties.
    4. Determine where a function is and is not continuous.
    5. Determine limits of functions at infinity.
    6. Determine limits of functions approaching vertical asymptotes.

    Readiness Assurance.

    Before beginning this chapter, you should be able to...
    1. Use function notation and evaluate functions
      • Review: Khan Academy 1 
      • Practice:
        • Evaluate functions 2 
        • Evaluate functions from their graphs 3 
        • Function notation word problems 4 
    2. Find the domain of a function
      • Review: Khan Academy 5 
      • Practice: Determine the domain of functions 6 
    3. Determine vertical asymptotes, horizontal asymptotes, and holes (removable discontinuities) of rational functions
      • Review:
        • Discontinuities of rational functions 7 
        • Finding horizonal asymptotes 8 
      • Practice:
        • Rational functions: zeros, asymptotes, and undefined points 9 
        • Finding horizonal asymptotes 10 
    4. Perform basic operations with polynomials
      • Review:
        • Adding and subtracting polynomials 11 
        • Multiplying polynomials 12 
      • Practice:
        • Add polynomials 13 
        • Subtract polynomials 14 
        • Multiply binomials by polynomials 15 
    5. Factor quadratic expressions
      • Review: Khan Academy 16 
      • Practice: Factoring quadratics intro 17 
    6. Represent intervals using number lines, inequalities, and interval notation
      • Review: Varsity Tutor 18
